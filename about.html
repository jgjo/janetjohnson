My Research Focus

My research primarily focuses on immersive spatial experiences (mainly Augmented Reality and Virtual Reality). With continually evolving emerging technology in the field, I aim to understand how the unique affordances of both the medium and the technologies used can be best utilized to create compelling and intuitive experiences.

Under this broader theme, my work spans three areas. My current projects fall under one or more of these categories:

  • Collaboration and communication: I aim to understand how different interface elements in immersive experiences(single and multi-device) affect collaboration and communication between people.
  • Support for design and creative expression: Designing complex experiences for both virtual and augmented reality typically requires programming knowledge and causes a plethora of gaps in the design process. I am working towards creating a tool that combines the simplicity of low-fidelity prototyping methods (like paper prototyping and bodystorming) with the richness of high-fidelity interactive prototypes. While geared towards creating *for* AR/VR - the project is evolving towards understanding how to use the medium for creative expressions for designers and non-designers alike.
  • Healthcare: I also work closely with hospitals and clinicians in my research. My most recent work involved modeling family-member experiences in the ICU to create a framework to inform how and when technological interventions could be introduced. The project involved over 80 hours of ethnography, several interviews, experience journals, and co-design sessions. I am also currently working on understanding the different elements (both human and technological) to design an extended reality system for remote open surgery.

On integrating my research into large scale products, I believe my work under the collaboration and communication setting aims to provide design guidelines that can be extended to a number of different application areas. It promotes a deeper understanding of how people interact with emerging technology, how it changes communication, and how the unique affordances of the medium can best be used to create intuitive and enjoyable experiences between people. My work in understanding how emerging technologies can aid creative expression and healthcare applications have a more direct path to large-scale use, and I am working towards seeing these projects used outside of research.
